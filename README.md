# Graphic Scene

## Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?

I approach software design by first identifying the desired outcome and then breaking it into smaller, manageable components. For this project, I began by studying my still life reference image and identifying the individual objects, materials, lighting, and camera perspective needed to recreate it. Rather than attempting to build the entire scene at once, I designed each object independently before focusing on how they worked together as a complete composition. This approach made debugging easier and allowed me to make gradual improvements throughout development.

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project strengthened my understanding of spatial reasoning and visual composition in a three-dimensional environment. I became more comfortable manipulating object transformations, scaling, rotations, textures, lighting, and camera placement to achieve a desired visual result. I also gained experience balancing realism with simplicity, learning that small adjustments to lighting and materials can significantly improve the overall appearance of a scene.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

My design process followed an iterative workflow. I selected a reference image, identified the primitive shapes needed to recreate each object, positioned the objects to establish the overall composition, and then refined the scene through lighting, textures, and material adjustments. Throughout development I continually compared my rendered scene against the original reference image and made incremental improvements until the final composition closely matched the intended design.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking larger problems into smaller components is a strategy that can be applied to almost any software project. Whether developing graphics applications or traditional software, creating and validating individual pieces before integrating them into the complete system improves organization, reduces debugging time, and results in more maintainable code.

### How do I approach developing programs?

I approach programming by implementing one feature at a time and verifying that each feature functions correctly before introducing additional complexity. This allows me to isolate problems quickly and prevents multiple issues from becoming intertwined during development.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One strategy I relied on throughout this project was incremental testing. After implementing each object, texture, lighting adjustment, or camera modification, I immediately rebuilt and tested the application before moving forward. I also reused transformation logic across multiple objects, allowing me to efficiently position and scale primitives while maintaining consistency throughout the scene.

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was one of the most important aspects of completing this project. Nearly every component of the scene—including object placement, camera positioning, textures, and lighting—was refined multiple times before producing the desired result. Each milestone built upon previous work, allowing me to improve the overall quality of the scene while strengthening my understanding of OpenGL concepts.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project's completion?

Throughout the milestones, I became more comfortable organizing larger programs into logical sections and separating responsibilities between scene setup, rendering, lighting, materials, and object creation. Early in the course I focused primarily on making code function correctly. By the end of the project, I was paying much greater attention to readability, organization, reuse, and maintainability while also considering the visual quality of the final result.
